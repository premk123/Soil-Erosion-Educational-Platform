SOIL EROSION

What is Soil Erosion?

Soil erosion is the wearing away of the top layer of soil. It happens due to water, wind, and human activities. It reduces soil fertility and affects agriculture.

Causes of Soil Erosion

Water and wind are the main causes. Removing plants and trees increases erosion.

Main Reasons

  • Water
  • Wind
  • Ice
  • Gravity

Signs of Soil Erosion

Thin soil, exposed rocks, cracks in land, and reduced fertility.

Effects of Soil Erosion

  • Loss of nutrients
  • Poor soil quality
  • Increased runoff
  • Crop damage
  • Silt deposits

Prevention Methods

Plant trees, use mulching, and maintain vegetation cover.
